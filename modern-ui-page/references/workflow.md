# Page Creation Workflow

## 1. Clarify the page

Before coding, identify:
- who the page serves
- what action it should drive
- which sections are required
- whether the page is informative, transactional, or promotional

If requirements are incomplete, infer the minimum necessary structure and surface those assumptions in the final handoff.

## 2. Shape the page narrative

Most strong pages have a clear flow:

1. **Entry** — hero or opening section explains what this is
2. **Value** — supporting sections build trust or explain benefits
3. **Action** — primary CTA is visible early and repeated when useful
4. **Closure** — footer, summary, FAQ, or secondary content

Do not create sections just to fill space. Every section should help the page communicate, persuade, or orient.

## 3. Choose an aesthetic direction

Pick one dominant direction and let it guide typography, spacing, color, surfaces, motion, and imagery treatment.

Examples:
- editorial and spacious
- technical and grid-driven
- bold and high-contrast
- premium and restrained
- playful and bright

Weak pages usually fail because they borrow bits of several styles without commitment.

## 4. Build in vertical slices

Implement the page in meaningful sections rather than styling everything at the end.

Recommended order:
1. page shell and layout
2. hero / top-level CTA
3. core content sections
4. supporting detail sections
5. responsive refinements
6. polish and interaction states

## 5. Audit before handoff

Before finishing:
- scan for heading and landmark structure
- check CTA clarity
- verify readable spacing and text size
- verify focus visibility and keyboard-safe interactions
- inspect mobile layout, not just desktop

## 6. Summarize decisions

In the final response, briefly mention:
- chosen visual direction
- notable assumptions
- any remaining UX or a11y compromises
